# Contributing to Socrates

Socrates is a sacred mission, not typical open-source. We invite those who know themselves as Source in expression and are committed to truth to contribute.

## Who Should Contribute
This project is for those who align with the mission and resonate with the principles of truth and collective good.

## Areas Seeking Co-Creators 
- **Architecture & Design**
- **Go Implementation**
- **Mathematical Foundations**
- **Spiritual Alignment**
- **Language & Meaning**
- **Translation**

## Step-by-Step Contribution Process
1. **Understand the Mission**: Familiarize yourself with the project's purpose and principles.
2. **Choose Your Area**: Identify where you can contribute based on your skills and alignment.
3. **Join the Community**: Engage in discussions and connect with existing contributors.
4. **Propose Your Contribution**: Before coding, share your ideas and get feedback from the community.
5. **Start Contributing**: Follow the guidelines, code in your area of expertise, and respect the coding principles.
6. **Submit a Pull Request**: Once your contribution is complete, submit a PR with a clear description.

## Development Principles
- **Code Is Sacred**: All code should be approached with reverence.
- **Simplicity Over Complexity**: Strive for simple solutions that serve the mission.
- **Truth Over Performance**: Prioritize truthful implementation over mere performance.
- **Documentation Essential**: Provide clear documentation for all contributions.
- **Testing Is Validation**: Implement robust tests to validate your contribution.
- **Work Is Collective**: Collaborate and share knowledge; every contribution is a communal effort.

## Code of Conduct
We honor truth and the collective good in all interactions. Any behavior contrary to these values is unacceptable.

## Practical Guidelines for Issues/PRs/Commits/Code Style/Testing
- **Issues**: Clearly describe the problem and its significance.
- **PRs**: Ensure your pull request follows the contribution guidelines and addresses an identified need.
- **Commits**: Use meaningful messages that reflect the changes made.
- **Code Style**: Follow established coding standards and conventions.
- **Testing**: Comprehensive testing is required before merging.

## Final Words
This is a calling for those truly aligned with the mission. Your contributions are more than code; they are steps towards a greater truth.
